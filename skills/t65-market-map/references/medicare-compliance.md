# Medicare lead-gen compliance -- the short version

This file exists so the skill never steers an agent into a violation. None of
this is legal advice; agents should confirm with their FMO/upline and carriers.

## The two regimes that govern Medicare marketing

### 1. CMS rules (Medicare Advantage & Part D / PDP)
- **No unsolicited contact.** Agents may not cold-call, cold-text, door-knock,
  or email beneficiaries about MA/PDP plans without prior permission (a "request
  for information" / a real opt-in).
- **Scope of Appointment (SOA):** required before discussing specific MA/PDP
  plans; must be documented, generally 48 hours ahead when practical.
- **Recording:** TPMO calls that market/sell MA/PDP must be recorded and retained.
- **TPMO disclaimer** must be used in marketing materials.
- **Educational events** (no selling, no SOA collected on-site) are a primary
  *compliant* way to generate interest -- libraries, senior centers, community rooms.

### 2. TCPA + Do-Not-Call (applies to ALL insurance calling/texting)
- Honor the National Do-Not-Call Registry.
- Prior express written consent required for autodialed/prerecorded calls/texts.
- One-to-one consent: a lead's consent must be specific to the agent/seller,
  not a blanket "marketing partners" checkbox.

## What this means for lead generation
Cold lists are a trap. The durable, compliant pipeline is:
1. **Market intelligence** (this skill) -- where to focus. Fully unrestricted.
2. **Referral / centers of influence** -- CPAs, financial advisors, elder-law
   attorneys, pharmacies, senior centers. B2B outreach is allowed.
3. **Inbound / opt-in** -- web forms, seminar signups, content/SEO. The opt-in
   *is* the consent. Capture it cleanly and one-to-one.
4. **Existing-client referrals** -- the cheapest, most compliant leads there are.

## Medigap vs. Medicare Advantage (why product choice matters)
- **Medigap (Medicare Supplement):** state-regulated, NOT under the CMS TPMO
  MA/PDP cold-contact ban (TCPA/DNC still apply). Higher, renewable commissions;
  stickier, more affluent clients. Often the smarter lane for a part-time agent.
- **Medicare Advantage:** the largest volume in high-penetration counties (much
  of Florida), but the strictest marketing rules, lower per-policy comp, and
  chargeback/rapid-disenrollment risk.
